The afternoon sun cast long shadows across the university courtyard as Sarah hurried toward the library. She had exactly three hours before her thesis defense, and there were still several citations to verify. The oak trees lining the path had witnessed countless students in similar states of pre-presentation panic over the decades.

Professor Martinez had warned her that the committee would ask tough questions about her methodology. "They'll want to know why you chose qualitative interviews over surveys," he'd said. "Be ready to defend that choice." Sarah had rehearsed her answer dozens of times, but anxiety still gnawed at her.

The library's revolving door deposited her into the cool, quiet interior. Rows of books stretched toward the ceiling, their spines creating a colorful tapestry of human knowledge. She found her usual spot by the window and spread out her notes, papers covered in highlighter marks and marginal scribbles.

Three hours. She could do this.
